Intellectual property

What is intellectual property?

Intellectual property is referring to creations of the mind, What does that mean? Creations of mind is referring to things like inventions; literary and artistic work; designs and symbols, names and images used in commerce.

  1. Trademarks
    • Trademark consists of a recognizable sign, word, phrase or symbol that helps identify the source of a product or service, which can include logos, brand names or slogan. TradeMark example: Coca-Cola:
  2. Copyright
    • This is a way to protect someone who creates an original work. A original work can be a book, a play, or a website, among others. The Goal is of copyrights is to prevent unauthorized copying of your work. Example would be Lyrics to a song, a movie or even a photograph.
  3. Patents
    • A patent can be described as a exclusive right granted for an invention, process, or product that is new, useful and non obvious. Example is the telephone was patented by Alexander Graham in 1876

What are digital products?

intangible goods or services created, sold, and consumed primarily through the internet.

There are a ton of digital products available, but I'll focus on just a few. One key category is Software Applications. These encompass a wide range of categories, but essentially, they are sets of instructions, data, or programs used to operate computers and execute specific tasks. Software is the non-tangible component of computers, distinct from the physical hardware. It can be categorized into two main types: system software and application software.

Another one would be Digital Content , this category of Digital product range from e-books to music to videos and even podcasts that users can access on a digital platform.
