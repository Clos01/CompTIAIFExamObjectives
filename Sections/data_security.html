Importance of investing in security

Why invest in Security?

Private information, company secrets, financial data, computer equipment and items of national security are at risk if the security is not appropriate.

  1. Data capture and collection:
    • This refers to the process of gathering relevant information that can help with security. For instance, it could involve collecting data from system logs, user activity, or network traffic.
  2. Data correlation:
    • After collecting the data, it’s analyzed and linked together to gain deeper insights. This helps identify patterns or anomalies that might indicate security risks.
  3. Meaningful reporting:
    • Once the data is analyzed, it's presented in an understandable and actionable format. This allows decision-makers to quickly identify issues and take appropriate steps to address them.

What is the CIA Triad?

The Cia Triad is composed of several Components.

We will first go over Confidentiality. What does this actually mean? Well, let's say you give your information to a bank to get a loan. One of the things they might ask for is your Social Security number, which is not something you want to give out freely. In the context of the CIA Triad, confidentiality refers to protecting sensitive data so that only authorized people can access it. Businesses use techniques like encryption, passwords, and access controls to maintain Confidentiality, ensuring that only the right people can view or use the data—keeping information private and secure.

Second Component in the CIA Triad is Integrity. This ensures that information stay authentic and is trustworthy, basically means that the data is not altered or tampered with by unauthorized individuals. It Guarantees that the information received is exactly as it was inteded, preventing it from improperly modified, corrupted or destroyed. Techniques like hashing, checksums, and digital signatures are often used to maintain integrity, ensuring that changes to the data can be detected and verified. Essentially, integrity keeps information accurate and reliable.

We will cover the last one called Availability . It is when the company takes measures to ensure that data and systems are accessible whenever needed by authorized users. For example, if a company’s server goes down, Availability measures like backups, redundancy (multiple servers), and load balancing come into play to keep the system online. Availability also involves keeping systems up-to-date and protected against threats like Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, ensuring continuous access to information.
